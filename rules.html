Badminton Rules

Below are concise rules and guidelines for playing badminton. This covers basic play, scoring, service, faults, and common match regulations.

General Play

  1. Match formats: singles (1 vs 1) and doubles (2 vs 2). Mixed doubles consist of one male and one female player per side.
  2. A match is the best of three games. Each game is played to 21 points (rally scoring).
  3. A point is scored on every rally, regardless of which side served.
  4. If the score reaches 20–20, play continues until one side gains a 2-point lead (e.g., 22–20). If the score reaches 29–29, the side scoring the 30th point wins the game.
  5. Players change ends after each game, and in the third game they change ends when the leading score reaches 11 points.

Service Rules

  1. The server and receiver must stand within their respective service courts without touching the boundary lines until the service is made.
  2. The shuttle must be struck below the server's waist (defined as the lowest rib) and the racket head must point downwards at the moment of impact.
  3. The server and receiver must have both feet in contact with the court surface and remain stationary until the service is delivered.
  4. Serve direction: diagonal from server's service court to the opponent's corresponding service court.

Common Faults

  1. The shuttle lands outside the court boundaries.
  2. The shuttle passes through or under the net, or fails to pass over the net.
  3. Player touches the net or its supports with racket, person, or clothing.
  4. Player deliberately distracts an opponent (e.g., loud shouts as the shuttle is struck).
  5. Double hit: the shuttle is struck twice by the same player or team.
