Equipments

Overview of common badminton equipment.

Racket

Lightweight frame (typically 80–100g) with a long shaft and stringed head. Modern rackets are made from carbon fiber or graphite composites for strength and flex. Choose grip size and string tension based on playing style—higher tension for control, lower tension for power.

Shuttle (Shuttlecock)

Either feathered (competition) or synthetic/plastic (recreational). Feather shuttles give predictable flight and better feel but wear faster and are more expensive. Shuttle speed is chosen to match altitude and temperature.

Net

The net divides the court and is 1.55 meters high at the edges and 1.524 meters (5 feet) at the center. Nets are usually made from braided cord or synthetic mesh and hung between two posts with a white tape on top.

Court

A rectangular court 13.4m long and 6.1m wide for doubles (5.18m wide for singles). Surfaces vary (wood, synthetic) but should provide good grip and shock absorption. Lines mark service and boundary areas.

Shoes

Indoor court shoes with non-marking rubber soles are recommended. Look for lateral support, cushioning, and a low, grippy outsole for quick direction changes and reduced ankle injury risk.
